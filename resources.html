Calderdale NAS Facebook Group

The Calderdale NAS Facebook Group is a closed gorup which members of the Calderdale NAS can join. If you are on Facebook, please request to join the group and a member of Calderdale NAS will contact you and ask for your email address to match it with the database of members. PLEASE CHECK YOUR 'OTHER' MESSAGES FOLDER ON FACEBOOK. This is where the message from one of our admins will likely end up unless you are already friends with them. Here is a guide to accessing your other messages.

We ask that all members of the Facebook group abide by the rules which are listed below;

  • The purpose of this page is to provide a forum so members of Calderdale NAS can support each other, share events/ideas, discuss problems and frustrations and share positive experiences. The direct selling or endorsement of products is not allowed but personal member recommendations of ASD related products, services, links to other projects and positive experiences are welcomed as can be useful to fellow members. However it must be recognised that they are personal recommendations and have not been verified by Calderdale NAS.
  • Please ensure we respect each others confidentiality and that of our families and remember that many affected by autism are already socially vulnerable and may be experiencing stress or pressure in their everyday lives. The information contained within this group is not intended for reproduction outside of this membership but it is to be remembered that although this is a closed group we cannot guarantee that it is private.
  • Please do not include personal information, names of children, adults or anyone without their prior permission. Schools, services and professionals should not be named. Information should not allow individuals to be indentifiable. This can be considered libellous.
  • Messages must not be offensive or insulting, racist, homophobic, contain religious intolerance, obscene or discriminatory in any way.
  • Although groups on Facebook need to be monitored or moderated, there is no way of monitoring all its users at all times. Any information on social networks CANNOT be guaranteed to be secure and IS in the public domain.

Books

Categories

From an Autistic Perspective

The Reason I Jump by Naoki Higashida

A unique book written by the then 13 year old Japanse autistic Naoki Higashida asking for understanding and acceptance of a different way of perceiving the world.

The Curious Incident of the Dog in the Night-time by Mark Haddon

A fictional account of a teenager who describes himself as "a mathematician with some behavioral difficulties" who sets out to solve the mysterious murder of a neighbour's dog.

From a Medical Perspective

The Autistic Spectrum by Lorna Wing

A guide aimed at professionals and parents on all aspects of ASD. Detailed, but accessible.
